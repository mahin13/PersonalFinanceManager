1. Getting Started

1.1 App Overview

Personal Finance Manager is a mobile application designed to help you manage your personal finances efficiently. The app provides a comprehensive suite of tools for tracking bank account balances, recording deposits and withdrawals, managing credit card bills, and keeping track of pending payments — all from your smartphone.

1.2 Installation

The app is available as an Android APK. To install it on your device:

  1. Download the APK file provided to you.
  2. On your Android device, go to Settings → Security and enable Install from Unknown Sources (if not already enabled).
  3. Open the downloaded APK file and tap Install.
  4. Once installed, tap Open to launch the app.

1.3 Key Features

Multi-Account Support Track multiple bank accounts simultaneously with real-time balance calculations.
Transaction Tracking Record deposits and withdrawals with reasons, filtered by day, month, or year.
Credit Card Management Track credit card bills with automatic overdue detection and payment reminders.
Pending Payments Keep track of upcoming payments, loans, and bills with due date tracking.
Smart Notifications Get priority-based alerts for overdue bills and upcoming payments.
Data Privacy All your financial data is stored locally on your device for maximum privacy.

2. Account Setup

2.1 Creating an Account

When you first open the app, you'll need to create an account. The sign-up process has three simple steps.

Figure 2.1 — Sign Up: Step 1 of 3 (Personal Details)

Step 1: Personal Details

  1. From the Login screen, tap "Sign Up" at the bottom.
  2. Enter your Full Name.
  3. Enter your Email address.
  4. Select your Date of Birth using the date picker.
  5. Create a Password (minimum 6 characters).
  6. Re-enter your password in the Confirm Password field.
  7. Tap "Next" to proceed to Step 2.

Step 2: Bank Accounts

  1. Enter the number of bank accounts you want to add.
  2. For each account, enter the bank name (e.g., DBBL, Brac Bank, City Bank).
  3. Tap "Next" to proceed to Step 3.
Tip

You can always add more bank accounts later from the Profile screen. Add at least one account during sign-up to start tracking your finances right away.

Step 3: Credit Cards (Optional)

  1. Toggle the switch if you have credit cards.
  2. If yes, enter the number of credit cards.
  3. For each card, provide the Bank Name, Bill Generation Day (1-31), and Last Payment Day (1-31).
  4. Tap "Create Account" to complete registration.
Note

The Bill Generation Day is when your credit card statement is generated each month. The Last Payment Day is the deadline to pay your bill to avoid late fees.

2.2 Signing In

Figure 2.2 — Login Screen

To sign in to your account:

  1. Enter your registered Email address.
  2. Enter your Password.
  3. Optionally, toggle "Remember Me" to save your credentials for next time.
  4. Tap "Sign In" to access your dashboard.
Tip

Enable "Remember Me" to skip entering your credentials each time you open the app. Your email and password will be securely saved on your device.

2.3 Resetting Your Password

Figure 2.3 — Reset Password Screen

If you've forgotten your password:

  1. On the Login screen, tap "Forgot Password?".
  2. Enter your registered Email Address.
  3. Tap "Reset Password".
  4. A temporary password will be displayed on screen. Note it down carefully.
  5. Use the temporary password to log in.
  6. After logging in, you will be prompted to set a new password.
Important

The temporary password is shown only once. Make sure to note it down before navigating away from the screen.

3. Dashboard

3.1 Dashboard Overview

The Dashboard is your financial command center. It gives you an at-a-glance view of your total balance, recent activity, account balances, and any pending items that need your attention.

Figure 3.1 — Dashboard Overview

The Dashboard is organized into the following sections:

Section Description
Header Shows your profile avatar, greeting (changes based on time of day), notification bell, and logout arrow button.
Total Balance Displays the combined balance across all your bank accounts in BDT.
Period Filter Switch between Daily, Monthly, and Yearly views for deposit/withdrawal summaries.
Summary Cards Vertically stacked rows showing total deposits (green), total withdrawals (red), and — if you have credit cards — credit card costs (orange) for the selected period. Each row displays an icon badge on the left, the label, and the amount on the right.
Quick Actions Green "+ Deposit" and red "- Cost" buttons for quick transactions.
Account Balances Lists each bank account with its current balance.
Pending Bills Shows pending or overdue credit card bills (scroll down to view).
Pending Payments Shows upcoming payments and overdue items (scroll down to view).

3.2 Balance & Summary

The total balance at the top is automatically calculated from all your bank account transactions. Use the filter buttons to view your income and expenses for different time periods:

  • Daily — Shows transactions from today only.
  • Monthly — Shows transactions from the current month.
  • Yearly — Shows transactions from the current year.

The summary cards are displayed as vertically stacked rows below the period filter. Each row has a colored left border, an icon badge, the category label on the left, and the amount (in BDT) on the right. This layout ensures amounts are always fully visible, even for large numbers.

Card Icon Color What It Includes
Total Deposits + Green All incoming money (salary, transfers, etc.).
Total Withdrawals - Red Bank account expenses, credit card bill payments, and other outgoing money from bank accounts.
Credit Card Costs C Orange Expenses charged to a credit card (only visible if you have credit cards enabled).
Note

Credit card costs are tracked separately from withdrawals. When you pay off a credit card bill from a bank account, that payment appears under Total Withdrawals. When you charge an expense to a credit card, it appears under Credit Card Costs. This separation helps you understand how much you're spending on credit versus cash.

3.3 Notifications

Figure 3.2 — Notifications Panel

The notification bell in the top-right corner shows a red badge with the count of active alerts. Tap it to open the Notifications panel. Notifications are automatically generated for:

  • Overdue payments — Highlighted in red (urgent priority).
  • Pending bill payments — Shows days remaining until due.
  • Upcoming payment reminders — For pending items nearing their due date.
Tip

Check your notifications regularly to stay on top of upcoming bills and avoid late fees. Notifications are sorted by urgency, with the most critical ones at the top.

3.4 Quick Actions

The two large action buttons on the dashboard let you quickly record transactions:

  • + Deposit — Tap to navigate to the Deposit screen and record incoming money.
  • - Cost — Tap to navigate to the Withdrawal screen and record an expense.
Tip

Pull down on the dashboard to refresh all data and recalculate balances.

4. Deposits

4.1 Making a Deposit

Record incoming money (salary, transfers, etc.) through the Deposit screen.

Figure 4.1 — Deposit Screen
  1. From the Dashboard, tap the green "+ Deposit" button.
  2. Select the Bank Account where the money is being deposited using the dropdown.
  3. The Current Balance for the selected account is displayed in green.
  4. Enter the Deposit Amount in BDT.
  5. A New Balance preview will appear showing the updated balance after the deposit.
  6. Tap "Confirm Deposit" to record the transaction.
  7. A success message will appear. Tap "OK" to return to the Dashboard.
Note

The deposit amount must be a valid number greater than zero. The new balance is previewed in real-time as you type.

5. Withdrawals & Expenses

5.1 Recording an Expense

Track your spending by recording expenses through the Cost / Withdrawal screen.

Figure 5.1 — Cost / Withdrawal Screen
  1. From the Dashboard, tap the red "- Cost" button.
  2. Select an account from the "Select Account" dropdown. The picker shows both Bank Accounts and Credit Cards, separated by section headers.
  3. If a bank account is selected, the Current Balance is shown in red. If a credit card is selected, no balance is displayed (credit cards don't have a running balance in the app).
  4. Enter the Amount in BDT.
  5. Enter a Reason / Description for the expense (e.g., "Groceries", "Transport").
  6. Alternatively, tap a Quick Category button to auto-fill the reason.
  7. If a bank account is selected and you enter an amount, a New Balance preview appears. This preview is hidden for credit card expenses.
  8. Tap "Confirm Expense" to record the transaction.

5.2 Credit Card Expenses

You can now record expenses made on a credit card directly from the Cost screen. This is separate from credit card bill payments.

  • Credit cards appear in the account picker — labeled as "BankName (Credit Card)" under a "Credit Cards" section header.
  • No balance impact — Credit card expenses do not affect any bank account balance. They are tracked separately as spending on that card.
  • View on Credit Cards screen — All expenses recorded against a credit card appear in the collapsible "Transactions" section on the Credit Cards screen (see Section 7.4).
  • Shown separately on Dashboard — Credit card expenses appear under the "Credit Card Costs" summary card (orange) on the Dashboard, separate from bank account withdrawals. This card is only visible to users with credit cards enabled.
Note

During CC Bill Payment mode (when the "CC Bill Payment" quick category is selected), credit cards are hidden from the account picker. You pay bills from a bank account, not from another credit card.

5.3 Quick Categories

At the bottom of the Withdrawal screen, you'll find Quick Category buttons for common expense types:

Category Use For

Warning

If the withdrawal amount exceeds your current balance, the New Balance preview will turn red to warn you that the account will go into a negative balance. You can still proceed, but please review carefully.

6. Transactions

6.1 Viewing Transactions

The Transactions screen provides a complete history of all your deposits and withdrawals, organized by date.

Figure 6.1 — Transactions Screen

Each transaction entry shows:

  • Transaction Type — + Deposit (green) or - Withdrawal (red).
  • Account — Which bank account or credit card was used. Credit card transactions display as "CardName (CC)" to distinguish them from bank account transactions.
  • Reason — The description/category (for withdrawals).
  • Amount — Color-coded: green for deposits, red for withdrawals.
  • Time — When the transaction was recorded.

Transactions are grouped by date with the most recent shown first.

6.2 Filtering Transactions

Use the filter tabs at the top of the screen to narrow down your view:

Filter Shows
AllEvery transaction ever recorded
TodayTransactions from today only
This MonthTransactions from the current month
This YearTransactions from the current year
Tip

Pull down on the Transactions screen to refresh and load the latest transactions.

6.3 Editing a Transaction

Every transaction card has visible "Edit" and "Remove" buttons at the bottom. You can modify any transaction's details after it has been recorded.

  1. Find the transaction you want to edit in the transaction list.
  2. Tap the blue "Edit" button at the bottom of the transaction card.
  3. The Edit Transaction modal will open with all the current details pre-filled.
  4. Modify any of the following fields:
    • Type — Toggle between Deposit and Withdrawal using the toggle buttons. The active type is highlighted in its respective color.
    • Account — Change which bank account this transaction belongs to using the dropdown picker. For credit card transactions, the credit card name is shown as a read-only label (e.g., "DBBL Visa (Credit Card)") and cannot be changed to a bank account.
    • Amount (BDT) — Update the transaction amount. Must be greater than zero.
    • Date — Change the transaction date in YYYY-MM-DD format (e.g., 2026-02-15). The original time of the transaction is preserved.
    • Reason — Update the description or reason for the transaction.
  5. Tap "Save" to apply your changes, or "Cancel" to discard them.
Tip

Made a deposit by mistake instead of a withdrawal? Use the Type toggle in the Edit modal to switch it without having to delete and re-create the transaction.

Note

Editing a transaction will immediately update your account balances and transaction summaries. The change is reflected everywhere in the app, including the Dashboard totals.

6.4 Removing a Transaction

You can permanently delete any transaction that was recorded incorrectly or is no longer needed.

  1. Find the transaction you want to remove in the transaction list.
  2. Tap the red "Remove" button at the bottom of the transaction card.
  3. A confirmation dialog will appear showing the transaction type and amount.
  4. Tap "Delete" to permanently remove the transaction, or "Cancel" to keep it.
Caution

Removing a transaction is permanent and cannot be undone. The deletion will immediately affect your account balance and all summary calculations. Double-check before confirming.

7. Credit Cards

7.1 Managing Credit Cards

The Credit Cards screen helps you keep track of all your credit card bills, payment history, and expenses made on each card. Navigate to this screen by tapping the "Credit Cards" tab in the bottom navigation bar.

Figure 7.1 — Credit Cards Screen

The screen is organized as follows:

  • Total CC Spending — A red-tinted summary card at the top showing the total amount spent across all credit cards (only visible if there are any CC transactions).
  • Per-Card Sections — Each credit card has its own card showing the bank name, bill/due dates, pending bills, payment history, and transactions.

Each credit card displays:

  • Bank Name — The name of the credit card (e.g., "DBBL Visa").
  • Bill Date | Due Date — When your statement is generated and when payment is due.
  • Total Pending — The total amount of unpaid bills (shown in orange if any are pending).
  • Active Bills — Pending and Overdue bills shown at the top with "Mark Paid" buttons.
  • Payment History — Collapsible section showing previously paid bills (see Section 7.3).
  • Transactions — Collapsible section showing expenses made on this card (see Section 7.4).

7.2 Tracking Bills

Bills have three possible statuses:

Status Color Meaning
Paid Green The bill has been paid. Moved to Payment History section.
Pending Orange The bill is due but hasn't been paid yet. A "Mark Paid" button is available.
Overdue Red The payment date has passed and the bill is still unpaid.

Active bills (Pending and Overdue) are shown prominently at the top of each card. To mark a bill as paid, tap the "Mark Paid" button next to the bill.

7.3 Payment History

Paid bills are separated from active bills into a collapsible "Payment History" section. This keeps the card view clean while still providing access to your bill payment records.

  • Tap "Payment History (N)" to expand or collapse the section, where N is the number of paid bills.
  • Each paid bill shows the bill month, a green "Paid" badge, the payment date, and the amount with strikethrough styling.
  • The payment date is derived from when the bill was marked as paid.

7.4 Credit Card Transactions

Each credit card now shows a collapsible "Transactions (N)" section listing all expenses recorded against that card from the Cost screen (see Section 5.2).

  • Tap "Transactions (N)" to expand or collapse the list, where N is the number of transactions.
  • Each transaction shows the reason/description, date, and amount in red.
  • These transactions are also visible on the main Transactions screen (Section 6), where they display the credit card name with a "(CC)" suffix.
Tip

Use the Transactions section to review what you've spent on each credit card. This helps you understand your CC spending before your bill statement arrives.

7.5 Adding Cards & Bills

Adding a New Credit Card

  1. Tap "+ Add" in the top-right corner of the Credit Cards screen.
  2. Enter the Bank Name (e.g., "DBBL Visa").
  3. Select the Bill Generation Day (the day of the month your statement is generated).
  4. Select the Last Payment Day (the deadline for payment).
  5. Tap "Add Card" to save.

Adding a New Bill

  1. On the desired credit card, tap "+ Add Bill".
  2. Enter the Bill Amount in BDT.
  3. Tap "Add Bill" to save.
Tip

Add your credit card bills as soon as you receive your statement. This helps you track payment deadlines and avoid overdue fees. The app will automatically notify you when bills are approaching their due date.

8. Pending Items

8.1 Managing Pending Items

The Pending Items screen helps you track upcoming payments, loans, and any other financial obligations. Navigate here by tapping the "Pending" tab in the bottom navigation bar.

Figure 8.1 — Pending Items Screen

Each pending item shows:

  • Title — Name of the payment or obligation.
  • Status Badge — Color-coded: Pending (orange), Overdue (red), or Completed (green).
  • Amount — The amount due in BDT.
  • Due Date — When the payment is due.
  • Days Remaining — How many days until (or since) the due date.
  • Description — Optional additional details (shown in italics).

Filter pending items using the tabs at the top:

Filter Shows
AllAll pending items regardless of status
PendingOnly items that are still pending
OverdueOnly items past their due date
CompletedOnly items marked as complete

8.2 Adding Pending Items

  1. Tap "+ Add" in the top-right corner.
  2. Enter a Title (e.g., "Rent Payment", "Internet Bill").
  3. Enter the Amount in BDT.
  4. Select the Due Date using the date picker.
  5. Optionally, add a Description for more details.
  6. Tap "Add Item" to save.

8.3 Editing Pending Items

You can edit any pending or overdue item to update its details.

  1. Find the pending item you want to edit in the list.
  2. Tap the orange "Edit" button on the item card.
  3. The Edit Pending Item modal will open with all current details pre-filled: Title, Amount, Due Date, and Description.
  4. Modify any of the fields as needed.
  5. Tap "Save Changes" to apply your edits, or "Cancel" to discard them.
Tip

Use the Edit option to update the due date or amount if your payment terms change. When editing, the date picker allows selecting past dates so you can correct an existing item's due date without restrictions.

Note

The Edit option is also available on the Dashboard in the "Pending Payments" section for quick access. Completed items cannot be edited.

8.4 Status Management

Each pending item has action buttons to manage its status:

  • Mark Complete — Tap to mark an item as paid/completed. The item will move to the "Completed" filter and appear with reduced opacity.
  • Edit — Tap to modify the item's title, amount, due date, or description (see Section 8.3).
  • Delete — Tap to permanently remove an item. A confirmation dialog will appear before deletion.
Important

Items that pass their due date are automatically marked as "Overdue" and highlighted with a red border. Keep track of overdue items to avoid financial penalties.

9. Profile & Settings

9.1 Profile Management

Access your profile by tapping on your profile avatar (the circle with your initials) in the top-left corner of the Dashboard.

Figure 9.1 — Profile Screen

From the Profile screen, you can:

  • Change Profile Picture — Tap the "Edit" badge on your avatar to select a photo from your gallery.
  • Edit Your Name — Tap on your name (where it says "Tap to edit") to open the name editing dialog.
  • View Your Email — Your registered email is displayed below your name.

9.2 Managing Bank Accounts

The Profile screen also lets you manage your bank accounts and credit cards:

Bank Accounts

  • Add Account — Tap "+ Add" next to "Bank Accounts", enter the bank name, and tap "Add".
  • Delete Account — Tap the red "X" next to any account. A confirmation dialog will appear. Note: Deleting an account also removes all its associated transactions.

Credit Cards

  • Add Card — Tap "+ Add" next to "Credit Cards", fill in the bank name and billing dates, and tap "Add".
  • Delete Card — Tap the red "X" next to any card. This also removes all associated bills and credit card transactions.
Caution

Deleting a bank account will permanently remove all transactions associated with that account. This action cannot be undone. Deleting a credit card will also remove all its bill records and any expenses recorded against that card.

9.3 Data Security

At the bottom of the Profile screen, you'll find the Data Security information section. Your financial data is stored entirely on your device using local storage. No data is sent to external servers, ensuring complete privacy and security of your financial information.

Note

Since all data is stored locally, uninstalling the app will permanently delete all your financial records. Consider keeping a backup of important data.

Logging Out

To log out, tap the red "Logout" button at the bottom of the Profile screen, or the arrow icon in the top-right corner of the Dashboard. A confirmation dialog will appear before you are logged out.
