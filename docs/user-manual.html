1. Getting Started

1.1 App Overview

Personal Finance Manager is a mobile application designed to help you manage your personal finances efficiently. The app provides a comprehensive suite of tools for tracking bank account balances, recording deposits and withdrawals, managing credit card bills, importing/exporting data, and keeping track of pending payments — all from your smartphone.

1.2 Installation

The app is available as an Android APK. To install it on your device:

  1. Download the APK file provided to you.
  2. On your Android device, go to Settings → Security and enable Install from Unknown Sources (if not already enabled).
  3. Open the downloaded APK file and tap Install.
  4. Once installed, tap Open to launch the app.

1.3 Key Features

Multi-Account Support Track multiple bank accounts simultaneously with real-time balance calculations.
Transaction Tracking Record deposits and withdrawals with reasons, filtered by day, month, or year.
Credit Card Management Track credit card bills with partial payments, minimum due, and overdue detection.
Pending Payments Keep track of upcoming payments, loans, and bills with due date tracking.
Smart Insights Data-driven financial insights with budget tracking, spending trends, and progress bars.
Import & Export Import transactions from CSV, TXT, JSON, or Excel files. Export data as Excel spreadsheets.
Smart Notifications Get priority-based alerts for overdue bills and upcoming payments.
Data Privacy All your financial data is stored locally on your device for maximum privacy.

  • What's New Auto-Popup — Existing users who update the app see a "What's New" popup on first launch showing new features.
  • Deposit Reason & Categories — Add a reason/source when making deposits, with quick category chips (Salary, Freelance, Gift, etc.).
  • Budget Settings — Set default monthly cost and deposit targets during sign-up or from the Profile screen.

2. Account Setup

2.1 Creating an Account

When you first open the app, you'll need to create an account. The sign-up process has four steps.

Figure 2.1 — Sign Up: Step 1 of 4 (Personal Details)

Step 1: Personal Details

  1. From the Login screen, tap "Sign Up" at the bottom.
  2. Enter your Full Name.
  3. Enter your Email address.
  4. Select your Date of Birth using the date picker.
  5. Create a Password (minimum 6 characters).
  6. Re-enter your password in the Confirm Password field.
  7. Tap "Next" to proceed to Step 2.

Step 2: Bank Accounts

  1. Enter the number of bank accounts you want to add.
  2. For each account, enter the bank name (e.g., DBBL, Brac Bank, City Bank).
  3. Tap "Next" to proceed to Step 3.
Tip

You can always add more bank accounts later from the Profile screen. Add at least one account during sign-up to start tracking your finances right away.

Step 3: Credit Cards (Optional)

  1. Toggle the switch if you have credit cards.
  2. If yes, enter the number of credit cards.
  3. For each card, provide the Bank Name, Bill Generation Day (1-31), and Last Payment Day (1-31).
  4. Tap "Next" to proceed to Step 4.
Note

The Bill Generation Day is when your credit card statement is generated each month. The Last Payment Day is the deadline to pay your bill to avoid late fees.

Step 4: Budget Settings & Default Accounts (Optional)

  1. Enter your Default Monthly Cost — the amount you typically spend per month. This is used for budget tracking in Insights.
  2. Enter your Default Monthly Deposit — the amount you typically earn/receive per month. This is used for income tracking in Insights.
  3. Default Deposit Account — Select which bank or MFS account to use by default when making quick deposits from the dashboard.
  4. Default Cost Account — Select which bank or MFS account to use by default when recording quick expenses from the dashboard.
  5. All fields are optional. You can skip this step by tapping "Skip".
  6. Tap "Create Account" to complete registration.
Tip

Setting budget values and default accounts during sign-up enables budget tracking in Insights and faster quick entries from the Dashboard. You can always update these later from the Profile screen.

2.2 Signing In

Figure 2.2 — Login Screen

To sign in to your account:

  1. Enter your registered Email address.
  2. Enter your Password.
  3. Optionally, toggle "Remember Me" to save your credentials for next time.
  4. Tap "Sign In" to access your dashboard.
Tip

Enable "Remember Me" to skip entering your credentials each time you open the app. Your email and password will be securely saved on your device.

2.3 Resetting Your Password

Figure 2.3 — Reset Password Screen

If you've forgotten your password:

  1. On the Login screen, tap "Forgot Password?".
  2. Enter your registered Email Address.
  3. Tap "Reset Password".
  4. A temporary password will be displayed on screen. Note it down carefully.
  5. Use the temporary password to log in.
  6. After logging in, you will be prompted to set a new password.
Important

The temporary password is shown only once. Make sure to note it down before navigating away from the screen.

Figure 3.1 — Dashboard Overview

The Dashboard is organized into the following sections:

Total Balance Displays the combined balance across all your bank accounts in BDT.
Period Filter Switch between Daily, Monthly, and Yearly views for deposit/withdrawal summaries.
Summary Cards Vertically stacked rows showing total deposits (green), total withdrawals (red), and credit card costs (orange) for the selected period.

3.2 Balance & Summary

The total balance at the top is automatically calculated from all your bank account transactions. Use the filter buttons to view your income and expenses for different time periods:

  • Daily — Shows transactions from today only.
  • Monthly — Shows transactions from the current month.
  • Yearly — Shows transactions from the current year.

The summary cards are displayed as vertically stacked rows below the period filter. Each row has a colored left border, an icon badge, the category label on the left, and the amount (in BDT) on the right.

Card Icon Color What It Includes
Total Deposits + Green All incoming money (salary, transfers, etc.).
Total Withdrawals - Red Bank account expenses, credit card bill payments, and other outgoing money.
Credit Card Costs C Orange Expenses charged to a credit card (only visible if you have credit cards).
Note

Credit card costs are tracked separately from withdrawals. When you pay off a credit card bill from a bank account, that payment appears under Total Withdrawals. When you charge an expense to a credit card, it appears under Credit Card Costs.

3.3 Notifications

Figure 3.2 — Notifications Panel

The notification bell in the top-right corner shows a red badge with the count of active alerts. Tap it to open the Notifications panel. Notifications are automatically generated for:

  • Overdue payments — Highlighted in red (urgent priority).
  • Pending bill payments — Shows days remaining until due.
  • Upcoming payment reminders — For pending items nearing their due date.
Tip

Check your notifications regularly to stay on top of upcoming bills and avoid late fees. Notifications are sorted by urgency, with the most critical ones at the top. Bills with only the minimum paid will continue to appear in notifications until fully paid.

3.4 Quick Actions

The dashboard provides quick access to recording transactions:

  • Import (small button, top-right) — Navigate to the Import Data screen to import transactions from a file.
  • + Deposit — Tap to navigate directly to the full Deposit screen.
  • - Cost — Tap to navigate directly to the full Cost/Withdrawal screen.

Quick Action Popup (Widget Only): The quick entry popup is triggered exclusively from the Quick Actions home screen widget (see Section 12.2). When you tap Deposit or Cost on the widget, the app opens with a quick popup that lets you:

  • Select from your bank/MFS accounts (your default account is pre-selected if configured)
  • Enter the amount
  • Add an optional reason
  • Tap "Open Full Page" at the bottom to switch to the detailed entry screen

Below the quick actions, you'll find the "Export Transactions as Excel" button that lets you export your transaction data as a downloadable Excel file (see Chapter 9).

Tip

Pull down on the dashboard to refresh all data and recalculate balances. Set your default accounts in Profile to speed up quick entries.

4. Deposits

4.1 Making a Deposit

Record incoming money (salary, transfers, etc.) through the Deposit screen.

Figure 4.1 — Deposit Screen
  1. From the Dashboard, tap the green "+ Deposit" button.
  2. Select the Bank Account where the money is being deposited using the dropdown.
  3. The Current Balance for the selected account is displayed in green.
  4. Enter the Deposit Amount in BDT.
  5. Enter a Reason / Source for the deposit (see Section 4.2).
  6. A New Balance preview will appear showing the updated balance after the deposit.
  7. Tap "Confirm Deposit" to record the transaction.
  8. A success message will appear. Tap "OK" to return to the Dashboard.

4.2 Deposit Reason & Categories

When making a deposit, you can specify the reason or source of the income. This helps you track where your money is coming from.

  • Enter a custom reason in the "Reason / Source" text field.
  • Or tap one of the Quick Category chips to auto-fill the reason:
Category Use For
SalaryMonthly salary or wages
FreelanceFreelance or contract income
GiftGifts or monetary presents
Investment ReturnReturns from investments
RefundRefunds from purchases
OtherAny other source of income
Tip

Adding a reason to your deposits helps the Financial Insights feature identify your most frequent income sources and provide better analysis.

Note

The deposit reason is optional. The deposit amount must be a valid number greater than zero. The new balance is previewed in real-time as you type.

5. Withdrawals & Expenses

5.1 Recording an Expense

Track your spending by recording expenses through the Cost / Withdrawal screen.

Figure 5.1 — Cost / Withdrawal Screen
  1. From the Dashboard, tap the red "- Cost" button.
  2. Select an account from the "Select Account" dropdown. The picker shows both Bank Accounts and Credit Cards, separated by section headers.
  3. If a bank account is selected, the Current Balance is shown in red. If a credit card is selected, no balance is displayed.
  4. Enter the Amount in BDT.
  5. Enter a Reason / Description for the expense (e.g., "Groceries", "Transport").
  6. Alternatively, tap a Quick Category button to auto-fill the reason.
  7. If a bank account is selected and you enter an amount, a New Balance preview appears.
  8. Tap "Confirm Expense" to record the transaction.

5.2 Credit Card Expenses

You can record expenses made on a credit card directly from the Cost screen. This is separate from credit card bill payments.

  • Credit cards appear in the account picker — labeled as "BankName (Credit Card)" under a "Credit Cards" section header.
  • No balance impact — Credit card expenses do not affect any bank account balance. They are tracked separately as spending on that card.
  • View on Credit Cards screen — All expenses recorded against a credit card appear in the collapsible "Transactions" section on the Credit Cards screen (see Section 7.6).
  • Shown separately on Dashboard — Credit card expenses appear under the "Credit Card Costs" summary card (orange) on the Dashboard.

5.3 Quick Categories

At the bottom of the Withdrawal screen, you'll find Quick Category buttons for common expense types:

Category Use For

If the withdrawal amount exceeds your current balance, the New Balance preview will turn red to warn you that the account will go into a negative balance. You can still proceed, but please review carefully.

6. Transactions

6.1 Viewing Transactions

The Transactions screen provides a complete history of all your deposits and withdrawals, organized by date.

Figure 6.1 — Transactions Screen

Each transaction entry shows:

  • Transaction Type — + Deposit (green) or - Withdrawal (red).
  • Account — Which bank account or credit card was used. Credit card transactions display as "CardName (CC)".
  • Reason — The description/category for the transaction.
  • Amount — Color-coded: green for deposits, red for withdrawals.
  • Time — When the transaction was recorded.

Transactions are grouped by date with the most recent shown first.

6.2 Filtering Transactions

Use the filter tabs at the top of the screen to narrow down your view:

Filter Shows
AllEvery transaction ever recorded
TodayTransactions from today only
This MonthTransactions from the current month
This YearTransactions from the current year
Tip

Pull down on the Transactions screen to refresh and load the latest transactions.

6.3 Editing a Transaction

Every transaction card has visible "Edit" and "Remove" buttons at the bottom. You can modify any transaction's details after it has been recorded.

  1. Find the transaction you want to edit in the transaction list.
  2. Tap the blue "Edit" button at the bottom of the transaction card.
  3. The Edit Transaction modal will open with all the current details pre-filled.
  4. Modify any of the following fields:
    • Type — Toggle between Deposit and Withdrawal.
    • Account — Change which bank account this transaction belongs to.
    • Amount (BDT) — Update the transaction amount.
    • Date — Change the transaction date in YYYY-MM-DD format.
    • Reason — Update the description or reason.
  5. Tap "Save" to apply your changes, or "Cancel" to discard them.
Note

Editing a transaction will immediately update your account balances and transaction summaries. The change is reflected everywhere in the app, including the Dashboard totals.

6.4 Removing a Transaction

You can permanently delete any transaction that was recorded incorrectly or is no longer needed.

  1. Find the transaction you want to remove in the transaction list.
  2. Tap the red "Remove" button at the bottom of the transaction card.
  3. A confirmation dialog will appear showing the transaction type and amount.
  4. Tap "Delete" to permanently remove the transaction, or "Cancel" to keep it.
Caution

Removing a transaction is permanent and cannot be undone. The deletion will immediately affect your account balance and all summary calculations.

7. Credit Cards

7.1 Managing Credit Cards

The Credit Cards screen helps you keep track of all your credit card bills, payment history, and expenses made on each card. Navigate to this screen by tapping the "Credit Cards" tab in the bottom navigation bar.

Figure 7.1 — Credit Cards Screen

The screen is organized as follows:

  • Total CC Spending — A red-tinted summary card at the top showing the total amount spent across all credit cards.
  • Per-Card Sections — Each credit card has its own card showing the bank name, bill/due dates, pending bills, payment history, and transactions.

Each credit card displays:

  • Bank Name — The name of the credit card (e.g., "DBBL Visa").
  • Bill Date | Due Date — When your statement is generated and when payment is due.
  • Total Pending — The total amount of unpaid bills.
  • Active Bills — Pending and Overdue bills with action buttons (Mark Paid, Edit, Pay Minimum).
  • Payment History — Collapsible section showing previously paid bills.
  • Transactions — Collapsible section showing expenses made on this card.

7.2 Tracking Bills

Bills have three possible statuses:

Status Color Meaning
Paid Green The bill has been fully paid. Moved to Payment History section.
Pending Orange The bill is due but hasn't been fully paid yet.
Overdue Red The payment date has passed and the bill is still unpaid or partially paid.

Active bills (Pending and Overdue) are shown prominently at the top of each card. Each active bill displays its amount, status, and any partial payment progress. If a minimum due amount was set, it is displayed below the bill amount.

7.3 Editing a Bill

You can edit the amount of any active (Pending or Overdue) bill:

  1. Find the active bill you want to edit on the Credit Cards screen.
  2. Tap the "Edit" button next to the bill.
  3. The Edit Bill modal will open with the current bill amount pre-filled.
  4. Enter the new Bill Amount.
  5. Tap "Save" to update the amount, or "Cancel" to discard changes.
Tip

Use the Edit feature when your credit card statement amount changes due to returns, adjustments, or corrections.

7.4 Partial & Minimum Payments

The app supports flexible payment options for credit card bills:

Full Payment

  1. Tap "Mark Paid" on the bill you want to pay.
  2. The payment mode defaults to "Full Payment".
  3. Select the bank account from which to deduct the payment.
  4. Tap "Confirm Payment" to pay the full bill amount.

Partial Payment

  1. Tap "Mark Paid" on the bill.
  2. Switch the payment mode to "Partial Payment".
  3. Enter the partial amount you want to pay.
  4. Toggle "Deduct from main bill?" to choose whether the partial payment should reduce the outstanding bill amount or just be recorded as a transaction.
  5. Select the bank account for the payment.
  6. Tap "Confirm Payment" to process.

Minimum Due Payment

If a minimum due amount was set when the bill was created, a "Pay Minimum" button appears on the bill. Tapping it pre-fills the partial payment amount with the minimum due value.

Important

Paying only the minimum due does not mark the bill as fully paid. The bill will remain in Pending or Overdue status, and notifications will continue until the full amount is paid.

When partial payments have been made on a bill, the display shows:

  • The original bill amount
  • The amount paid so far (in green)
  • The remaining balance
Note

When the "Deduct from main bill?" toggle is ON, partial payments reduce the outstanding bill amount. When it reaches zero, the bill is automatically marked as Paid. When the toggle is OFF, the payment is recorded as a transaction but the bill amount remains unchanged.

7.5 Payment History

Paid bills are separated from active bills into a collapsible "Payment History" section. This keeps the card view clean while still providing access to your bill payment records.

  • Tap "Payment History (N)" to expand or collapse the section.
  • Each paid bill shows the bill month, a green "Paid" badge, the payment date, and the amount with strikethrough styling.

7.6 Credit Card Transactions

Each credit card shows a collapsible "Transactions (N)" section listing all expenses recorded against that card from the Cost screen (see Section 5.2).

  • Tap "Transactions (N)" to expand or collapse the list.
  • Each transaction shows the reason/description, date, and amount in red.
  • These transactions are also visible on the main Transactions screen (Section 6), where they display the credit card name with a "(CC)" suffix.
  • "Pay" Button — Each unpaid transaction shows a blue "Pay" button. Tapping it opens a modal where you can select which bank account to pay from. The payment creates a withdrawal on the selected bank account and deducts the transaction amount from the card's most recent pending bill. Once the bill is fully covered, it is automatically marked as "Paid". Transactions already paid show a "Bill Paid" badge.

7.7 Adding Cards & Bills

Adding a New Credit Card

  1. Tap "+ Add" in the top-right corner of the Credit Cards screen.
  2. Enter the Bank Name (e.g., "DBBL Visa").
  3. Select the Bill Generation Day (the day of the month your statement is generated).
  4. Select the Last Payment Day (the deadline for payment).
  5. Tap "Add Card" to save.

Adding a New Bill

  1. On the desired credit card, tap "+ Add Bill".
  2. Enter the Bill Amount in BDT.
  3. Optionally, enter the Minimum Due amount — the minimum payment required to avoid late fees.
  4. Tap "Add Bill" to save.
Tip

Add your credit card bills as soon as you receive your statement. Include the minimum due amount so you can quickly make minimum payments when needed. The app will keep notifying you until the full bill is paid.

8.3 Editing Pending Items

You can edit any pending or overdue item to update its details.

  3. The Edit Pending Item modal will open with all current details pre-filled.
  4. Modify any of the fields as needed.
  5. Tap "Save Changes" to apply your edits, or "Cancel" to discard them.

8.4 Status Management

Each pending item has action buttons to manage its status:

  • Mark Complete — Tap to mark an item as paid/completed.
  • Edit — Tap to modify the item's details (see Section 8.3).
  • Delete — Tap to permanently remove an item.
Important

Items that pass their due date are automatically marked as "Overdue" and highlighted with a red border. Keep track of overdue items to avoid financial penalties.

9. Import & Export Data

9.1 Importing Transactions

The Import Data feature lets you bulk-import transactions from external files into the app. This is useful for migrating data from other finance apps or spreadsheets.

Supported File Formats

Format Extension Description
CSV .csv Comma-separated values. The most common format for spreadsheet exports.
Text .txt Tab, comma, semicolon, or pipe-delimited text files. The delimiter is auto-detected.
JSON .json JSON array of transaction objects, or an object with a transactions key.
Excel .xlsx, .xls Microsoft Excel spreadsheet files.

Download Template

Before importing, you can download a blank template file to see the expected format. On the Import screen, under "Step 1: Select File", you'll see a "Download Template" section with four buttons:

  • CSV — Comma-separated template with headers (date, type, amount, reason) and 2 sample rows.
  • TXT — Tab-separated template with the same structure.
  • JSON — JSON array of sample transaction objects.
  • XLSX — Excel spreadsheet template with headers and sample data.

Tap any format button to download the template via your device's share dialog. Fill in the template with your data and then import it.

Import Steps

  1. From the Dashboard, tap the orange "Import" button.
  2. Optionally, download a template (see above) to understand the expected file format.
  3. Tap "Choose File" and select a CSV, TXT, JSON, or Excel file from your device.
  4. The app will parse the file and display the detected columns with a preview of the first 5 rows.
  5. Map the columns: The app auto-detects common column names (type, amount, reason, date). You can manually adjust the mapping using the dropdown pickers if needed.
  6. Select the bank account to assign the imported transactions to.
  7. Review the preview and tap "Import Transactions" to complete the import.
  8. A success message will confirm how many transactions were imported.
Tip

For best results, ensure your file has headers like "type", "amount", "reason", and "date". The auto-mapping feature recognizes many common aliases (e.g., "description" for "reason", "category" for "reason", "value" for "amount").

Note

The "type" column should contain values like "deposit", "income", "credit" (for deposits) or "withdrawal", "expense", "debit", "cost" (for withdrawals). If the type is not recognized, the transaction defaults to "withdrawal".

9.2 Exporting Transactions

Export your transaction data as an Excel spreadsheet for backup or analysis in other tools.

  1. On the Dashboard, tap the "Export Transactions as Excel" button.

10.1 Profile Management

10.2 Budget Settings

The Budget Settings section on the Profile screen lets you set or update your default monthly financial targets. These values power the budget and income tracking features in Financial Insights.

  • Default Monthly Cost — Your expected monthly spending amount. When set, the Insights section will show a budget usage progress bar.
  • Default Monthly Deposit — Your expected monthly income amount. When set, the Insights section will show an income tracking progress bar.
  1. Scroll to the "Budget Settings" section on the Profile screen.
  2. Tap the "Edit" button.
  3. Enter or update your Default Monthly Cost and Default Monthly Deposit values.
  4. Tap "Save" to apply the changes.
Tip

These values are optional but highly recommended. Setting them enables powerful budget tracking insights on your Dashboard, helping you stay within your spending limits and track your income goals.

10.2.1 Default Accounts

The Default Accounts section (visible when you have bank accounts) lets you choose which accounts are pre-selected when using the quick deposit and cost popups on the Dashboard.

  • Default Deposit Account — Tap a bank/MFS name to set it as your default for quick deposits. Tap again to deselect.
  • Default Cost Account — Tap a bank/MFS name to set it as your default for quick expenses. Tap again to deselect.

Changes are saved automatically when you tap an account chip.

10.3 Managing Bank Accounts

The Profile screen also lets you manage your bank accounts and credit cards:

Bank Accounts

  • Add Account — Tap "+ Add" next to "Bank Accounts", enter the bank name, and tap "Add".
  • Delete Account — Tap the red "X" next to any account. Note: Deleting an account also removes all its associated transactions.

Credit Cards

  • Add Card — Tap "+ Add" next to "Credit Cards", fill in the bank name and billing dates, and tap "Add".
  • Delete Card — Tap the red "X" next to any card. This also removes all associated bills and transactions.
Caution

Deleting a bank account will permanently remove all transactions associated with that account. Deleting a credit card will also remove all its bill records and any expenses recorded against that card. These actions cannot be undone.

Automatic Update Popup: When you update the app to a new version, a "What's New" popup automatically appears on the Dashboard showing all new features. This only appears once per version update.

10.5 Data Security

Your financial data is stored entirely on your device using local storage. No data is sent to external servers, ensuring complete privacy and security of your financial information.

Note

Since all data is stored locally, uninstalling the app will permanently delete all your financial records. Use the Export feature (Section 9.2) to keep regular backups of your data.

Logging Out

To log out, tap the red "Logout" button at the bottom of the Profile screen, or the arrow icon in the top-right corner of the Dashboard. A confirmation dialog will appear before you are logged out.

12. Home Screen Widgets

The app includes two Android home screen widgets that give you quick access to key features without opening the full app.

12.1 Adding Widgets

To add a widget to your home screen:

  1. Long-press on an empty area of your Android home screen.
  2. Tap "Widgets" from the menu that appears.
  3. Scroll through the widget list and find "Finance Manager".
  4. You will see two widget options: "Finance - Quick Actions" and "Finance - Balance".
  5. Long-press your desired widget and drag it to your home screen.
  6. Resize the widget by dragging its edges if needed.

12.2 Quick Actions Widget

The Quick Actions widget provides two buttons on your home screen:

  • + Deposit — Tap to open the app and launch a quick Deposit popup on the Dashboard, allowing you to record a deposit without navigating away.
  • - Cost — Tap to open the app and launch a quick Cost popup on the Dashboard, allowing you to record a withdrawal without navigating away.

This widget is designed as a compact 3x1 cell widget, perfect for placing at the bottom or top of your home screen for instant access to recording transactions. Note: The quick action popup is exclusive to the widget — tapping the Deposit or Cost buttons inside the app will navigate directly to the full Deposit or Withdrawal screens.

12.3 Balance Widget

The Balance widget shows your financial overview at a glance:

  • Total Balance — Your combined balance across all bank accounts, displayed prominently.
  • Individual Accounts — Each bank account's name and current balance (up to 5 accounts).
  • Color-coded — Positive balances are shown in green, negative in red.

The widget automatically refreshes when you open the app or make any transaction. Tapping anywhere on the widget opens the full app.

Tip

The Balance widget updates every 30 minutes automatically, and immediately whenever you make a deposit or record an expense through the app. For the most up-to-date balance, simply tap the widget to open the app.

Note

Both widgets require you to be logged in to the app. If you log out and log back in, the widgets will update with your account data on the next refresh.
